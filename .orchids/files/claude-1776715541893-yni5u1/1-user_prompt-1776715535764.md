Build an interactive thread preview component for a website called "Chain" 
(retrieve-your-roots.com) — an educational site tracing the causal chain 
of Black history from African civilizations to present-day America.

The component should go on the homepage and show visitors what a "thread" 
looks like before they click into one. Use the COINTELPRO thread as the 
sample content.

## What a thread looks like
Each thread has:
- A title + era tag (e.g. "Civil Rights → Backlash · 1956–1971")
- An intro paragraph explaining the central argument
- A series of numbered entries, each with:
  - A date/year
  - A headline
  - A location
  - 2–3 stat chips (e.g. "15 yrs · Official duration", "2,000+ · Operations")
  - 2–3 paragraphs of narrative prose
  - Sometimes a pull quote
- A "The Longer Chain" footer linking to the next thread

## The component to build
A homepage section titled something like "Here's how a thread works" 
or "See it in action" that shows:

1. A collapsed thread preview — showing only the first 2 entries of 
   the COINTELPRO thread, with a "Continue reading →" that expands inline 
   to show entries 3–5
2. A progress bar or step indicator at the top (e.g. "Entry 2 of 5")
3. A "Read full thread" button at the bottom
4. A subtle fade/mask at the bottom of the collapsed state to 
   suggest more content below

## Visual style
- Dark background (#0a0a0a or similar near-black)
- Off-white body text (#e8e4d9 or similar warm white)  
- Muted amber or sepia accent color for dates, stat chips, and entry numbers
- Serif font for headlines (Georgia or similar), sans-serif for body
- Left border accent line on each entry (thin, amber/sepia)
- Clean, editorial — think longform journalism, not SaaS dashboard
- Mobile responsive

## Sample content to use (COINTELPRO thread)

Title: COINTELPRO: The FBI's War on Black America
Era: Civil Rights → Backlash · 1956–1971
Intro: "From 1956 to 1971, the FBI ran a classified program specifically 
designed to 'expose, disrupt, misdirect, discredit, or otherwise neutralize' 
Black political organizations. Its targets included the NAACP, Martin Luther 
King Jr., the Nation of Islam, and the Black Panther Party. Its methods 
included infiltration, forged letters, planted evidence, and coordinating 
police assassinations. This is why the Civil Rights movement did not produce 
lasting institutional change."

Entry 1 — August 1956
Headline: Hoover's Memo: "Expose, Disrupt, Neutralize"
Location: FBI Headquarters, Washington D.C.
Stats: 15 yrs · Official duration | 2,000+ · Documented operations
Body: On August 28, 1956, FBI Director J. Edgar Hoover sent a memo to field 
offices authorizing a new secret counterintelligence program. Its first major 
Black target was the NAACP — the oldest and most mainstream civil rights 
organization in the country. The program's stated goal: "prevent the rise of 
a 'messiah' who could unify and electrify the militant Black nationalist movement."

Entry 2 — 1956–1971
Headline: The Tactics: How You Destroy a Movement from the Inside
Location: Nationwide
Stats: Infiltration | Forged Letters | Fake News Plants | Assassination
Body: FBI informants joined organizations at every level to instigate 
internal conflicts. The FBI forged letters between Black leaders to create 
suspicion and rivalry. In the most extreme cases, the FBI coordinated 
directly with local police to kill Black Panther leaders.

Entry 3 — 1963–1968
Headline: Martin Luther King Jr.: "The Most Dangerous Negro in America"
Location: Washington D.C. · Atlanta · Memphis
Body: After King's "I Have a Dream" speech, Hoover wrote that King was 
"the most dangerous Negro in America." The FBI wiretapped his phones, 
bugged his hotel rooms, and sent him an anonymous letter urging suicide — 
approved at the highest levels of the FBI.

Entry 4 — December 4, 1969
Headline: The Assassination of Fred Hampton
Location: Chicago, Illinois
Stats: 21 · Hampton's age | 4:45 am · Time of raid | 99 · Shots fired (1 by occupants)
Body: Fred Hampton was the 21-year-old chairman of the Illinois Black Panther 
Party. FBI informant William O'Neal had infiltrated Hampton's inner circle 
and provided a detailed floor plan of his apartment. At 4:45 a.m., Chicago 
police — briefed by the FBI — raided the apartment. 99 shots were fired; 
98 came from police.

Entry 5 — 1971–Present
Headline: The Break-In That Exposed Everything
Location: Media, Pennsylvania
Body: In 1971, activists broke into the FBI field office in Media, PA and 
stole over 1,000 documents, revealing COINTELPRO for the first time. The 
Church Committee confirmed the program's scope in 1975. No agent was ever 
prosecuted. The program was never dismantled — it was renamed.
