Know a story that
belongs in the chain?

Chain is built on the principle that history is causal — that present conditions have specific, traceable origins. If you know a thread of history that's been overlooked, misrepresented, or left unconnected to the present, tell us about it.

Submit your thread idea

Tell us the core causal claim, the historical starting point, and how it connects to a present-day condition. We review every submission and respond within two weeks.

About you

We welcome submissions from everyone — no credentials required.

The thread

A working title is fine — we'll refine it during editorial review.

This is the core of a Chain thread — the causal link between a past decision and a present reality.

Sources

Chain is a sourced platform. We can't consider submissions without at least two verifiable sources.

We review every submission personally. You'll receive a response within two weeks. We may reach out for more information before making a decision.

What makes a good Chain thread?

  • ⛓

    Causal, not just chronological

    A thread shows how A directly caused B caused C. Not just "things that happened" in sequence.

  • 📍

    Connects to the present

    Every Chain thread ends in a present-day condition — a wealth gap, a policy, a pattern that still exists.

  • 📚

    Grounded in primary sources

    We don't publish threads that can't be sourced. Books, academic papers, and primary documents are all valid.

  • 🔍

    Overlooked or misrepresented

    Chain focuses on stories that mainstream history education obscures — we're not building a general encyclopedia.

Not sure if your idea fits? Send it anyway — or contact us first to discuss. We'd rather hear too many ideas than miss a good one.
